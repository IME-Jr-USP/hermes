# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Hermes serves students from across the Universidade de Sao Paulo, primarily undergraduates aged 19 to 23 who need clearer access to academic information. They use the product while exploring disciplines, understanding curriculum structures, comparing electives, and making academic decisions with limited time and incomplete context.

## Product Purpose

Hermes is an AI agent that helps USP students ask natural-language questions about disciplines, course content, curriculum paths, and academic opportunities. The initial MVP is a single chat page with message history for the current session, a text input, and a send action.

The product exists to reduce the friction caused by scattered institutional systems, unintuitive pages, and informal knowledge gaps between newer and more experienced students. Success means students can quickly understand relevant academic options and leave the conversation more confident about their next decision.

## Brand Personality

Hermes should feel modern, clear, technological, elegant, and minimalist. The interface should be calm and intentional, with a high-quality conversational feel inspired by Claude: refined spacing, precise interaction states, and purposeful motion.

The voice should be helpful, direct, and academically grounded. It should feel like a capable guide for university decisions, not a generic assistant trying to impress the user.

## Anti-references

Hermes must not look chaotic, polluted, noisy, or overdecorated. Avoid generic AI dashboard patterns, excessive gradients, busy decorative effects, novelty chatbot styling, and interfaces that feel like AI-generated SaaS templates.

The product should not bury the chat experience under unnecessary navigation, cards, metrics, history panels, or feature marketing. For the MVP, the chat is the product.

## Design Principles

1. Make the conversation central.
2. Reduce academic uncertainty without adding interface complexity.
3. Keep every surface calm, readable, and intentional.
4. Use motion to clarify state changes, never to decorate.
5. Build trust through restraint, clarity, and predictable behavior.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Hermes should target WCAG AA for contrast, focus visibility, and interaction clarity. The MVP chat flow should be fully keyboard accessible, with visible focus states, readable text sizing, and clear send/disabled/loading states.

The interface should support reduced-motion preferences and avoid relying on color alone to communicate meaning. Portuguese should be treated as the primary language context, with copy that is clear for students from different USP programs and levels of familiarity with academic systems.
